I grew up in Mississauga, shaped by Arabic heritage, educated in English, driven enough to develop French as a third language. Not because I had to — because being globally capable is a competitive advantage.

At 16, my family relocated to Dubai. No friends. No social safety net. No expectations to hide behind. The move was the most formative thing that has happened to me — a forced confrontation with who I actually was beneath comfort and familiarity.

Stripped of labels, I rebuilt deliberately. That process gave me clarity about my values, my goals, and what I am actually capable of.

What I am today is not a coincidence. I read across philosophy, finance, strategy, history — not because I was assigned to, but because I cannot stop. I play chess seriously. I train physically with the same intensity I bring to ideas. I think in systems and phases, not impulses.

Formative encounters

The real classroom.

Two experiences outside the academic frame shaped me more than any course. One taught me discipline; the other taught me how a business actually functions beneath the surface.

2019 — 2020 · Mississauga, ON
Canadian Army Cadet
Royal Canadian Army Cadet Corps

Immersed in an environment built on discipline, structure, and leadership. The program demanded punctuality, physical fitness, teamwork, and the ability to both follow and give direction. I learned how high-performing teams operate under pressure — and that leadership is earned through consistency and reliability, never granted by title.

2024 · Dubai, UAE
Operations Management — Shadow
Restaurant Industry · Thai Concept

Shadowed an Operations Manager and observed firsthand how a business functions at ground level — staff coordination, inventory and supply chain, customer experience, daily decision-making. What struck me most was how many moving parts had to align simultaneously, and how the best operators made it look seamless through anticipation rather than reaction.
